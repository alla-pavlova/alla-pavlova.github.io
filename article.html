From structured workflows to backend systems

My path into software development is not a typical one.

My path into software development is not a typical one. Before moving into IT, I worked in environments that required structured thinking, coordination, and working with complex processes. Over time, I realized that many of these skills directly translate into backend development. What I enjoy most about backend engineering is working with systems as a whole — understanding how data flows, how services interact, and how to build something that is reliable and maintainable. Today I work with FastAPI, ASP.NET, and React, building web applications and APIs. I focus on clear structure, clean logic, and practical solutions rather than overcomplicated designs. One of the areas that interests me the most is building applied systems — OCR services, data processing pipelines, and backend logic that solves real problems. I am also interested in cloud deployment and infrastructure basics, using Docker, AWS, and Terraform to understand how applications move from development to production. My goal is to grow as a backend developer and work on real products where system thinking, reliability, and practical engineering matter. I am open to backend roles, internships, and opportunities to grow in a professional development environment.
